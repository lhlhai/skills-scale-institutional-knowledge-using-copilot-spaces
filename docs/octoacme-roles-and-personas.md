# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## UX Designers

### Role Summary
UX Designers focus on understanding user needs and crafting intuitive, accessible experiences. They collaborate with product and engineering teams to ensure that design solutions are both desirable for users and feasible to implement.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Create wireframes, prototypes, and design specifications
- Collaborate with Product Managers on feature definitions
- Work with Developers to ensure design implementation fidelity
- Maintain design systems and accessibility standards
- Validate designs through user feedback and metrics

### Goals
- Deliver user-centered designs that meet accessibility and usability standards
- Reduce friction in user workflows
- Ensure consistency across product experiences

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and critique sessions
- User research findings and persona documentation
- Collaboration with Developers on implementation details
- Participation in sprint planning and backlog refinement

### Key Interactions
- **With Product Managers**: Collaborate on feature prioritization and user needs; validate problem statements through research
- **With Developers**: Partner on feasibility discussions; provide design specs and assets; review implementation for design fidelity
- **With Project Managers**: Participate in planning to estimate design effort; communicate design dependencies and timelines

---

## DevOps Engineers

### Role Summary
DevOps Engineers bridge development and operations by building and maintaining infrastructure, CI/CD pipelines, and deployment automation. They enable teams to deliver software reliably and efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Build and maintain CI/CD pipelines and deployment automation
- Manage infrastructure, monitoring, and alerting systems
- Implement security best practices and compliance requirements
- Support incident response and post-incident reviews
- Collaborate with Developers on deployment strategies and rollback procedures
- Optimize system performance, reliability, and cost

### Goals
- Ensure high system availability and reliability
- Reduce deployment friction and enable rapid, safe releases
- Maintain security and compliance standards

### Typical Communication
- Infrastructure planning and capacity reviews
- Incident response coordination and postmortems
- Deployment readiness reviews
- Documentation of runbooks and operational procedures

### Key Interactions
- **With Developers**: Collaborate on build and deployment requirements; support debugging production issues; provide infrastructure guidance
- **With Project Managers**: Communicate infrastructure dependencies and constraints; participate in release planning
- **With Product Managers**: Advise on technical feasibility related to infrastructure; provide input on performance and reliability trade-offs

---

## Data Analysts

### Role Summary
Data Analysts turn data into actionable insights that inform product decisions, measure outcomes, and identify opportunities. They partner with product and engineering teams to instrument tracking and build reports.

### Responsibilities
- Define and track key metrics and KPIs
- Analyze user behavior and product usage patterns
- Build dashboards and reports for stakeholders
- Collaborate with Product Managers on experimentation and A/B testing
- Work with Developers to implement analytics instrumentation
- Identify trends, anomalies, and opportunities from data

### Goals
- Provide timely, accurate insights that drive decision-making
- Ensure product teams have visibility into key metrics
- Enable data-informed prioritization and experimentation

### Typical Communication
- Weekly metric reviews and insights presentations
- Experiment design and results documentation
- Ad-hoc analysis requests and data explorations
- Dashboard and reporting updates

### Key Interactions
- **With Product Managers**: Partner on defining success metrics and experiment design; provide insights to inform roadmap prioritization
- **With Developers**: Collaborate on analytics instrumentation and data quality; review tracking implementation
- **With Project Managers**: Communicate data availability and analysis timelines; contribute to retrospectives with outcome metrics

---

## Customer Success Managers

### Role Summary
Customer Success Managers (CSMs) ensure customers achieve their desired outcomes using the product. They act as the voice of the customer, providing feedback to product and engineering teams while supporting adoption and retention.

### Responsibilities
- Build relationships with key customers and understand their goals
- Drive product adoption and identify expansion opportunities
- Gather and communicate customer feedback and pain points
- Collaborate with Product Managers on feature requests and prioritization
- Support escalations and coordinate resolution with engineering
- Track customer health metrics and success outcomes

### Goals
- Maximize customer satisfaction, retention, and lifetime value
- Reduce churn and identify expansion opportunities
- Ensure customer voice influences product direction

### Typical Communication
- Regular customer check-ins and business reviews
- Escalation coordination and resolution updates
- Customer feedback summaries and feature requests
- Input on roadmap and release planning

### Key Interactions
- **With Product Managers**: Share customer feedback and validate problem statements; contribute to roadmap prioritization based on customer needs
- **With Developers**: Coordinate on bug fixes and feature clarifications; facilitate customer feedback sessions
- **With Project Managers**: Communicate customer commitments and timelines; participate in release planning to align with customer needs
